Steppe и Tayga:

longest train journey in the world

Pies with liver, demobilization, conductors Tanya and Nadya, fermented baked milk at the stations, Khabarovsk, Izhevsk, Moscow - only 18 days on the way. This is how musician David Bowie made his way to Europe after a tour of Japan in the spring of 1973. Bowie was afraid to fly, so he took the train.

First - the familiar landscapes of the European part of Russia: fields, summer cottages, villages, forest belts, power lines. Abramtsevo, Berendeevo, Rostov Veliky, Nerekhta: it seems that either in the country of hobbits, or in the past, in 1973, and now from a neighboring the coupe will be released by a tall, awkward man in a Japanese kimono and bright pants.

It's time to improve your everyday life. In Kostroma, you bought everything that you forgot to take with you - instant coffee, vodka, doshik. Make yourself comfortable. We have not even reached the Urals yet.

The journey finally ceased to be a journey and became a trip. This mystical you will never forget the experience of immersion in the depths of Russia. Europe is left behind but Asia does not even think of starting. Perhaps you have stood still all this time. FROM on the other hand, if you look out the window for a long time (and what else to do), you can see the army Kolchak, attacking the Reds. Or mammoths that once roamed here along rail.

Baikal is behind. You are in the heart of the continent, there is no Hellene, no Jew, no Europe, not Asia. The train passes the highest point of the Transsib, after which it begins path to the sea. Once the road narrowed here to one track and went through a narrow tunnel, on the portal of which there was a chthonic indication of the direction: "To the great ocean."

Khabarovsk appears when it already seems that you will spend the rest of your life on the train. ABOUT you already know more about your neighbors in the carriage than about your next of kin. But also some 800 kilometers - and Vladivostok.

Vladivostok

It's like a trip to the Zone in Tarkovsky's Stalker. Something different happens to everyone but, having made this path, no one remains the same person who left Moscow a week ago.
